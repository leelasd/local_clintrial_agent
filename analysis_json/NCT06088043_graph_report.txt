## Clinical Trial Design Assessment Report: NCT06088043

### 1. Trial Overview and Design

**Trial Title:** A Study About How Well TAK-279 Works and Its Safety in Participants With Moderate-to-severe Plaque Psoriasis During 52 Weeks of Treatment.

**Phase:** Phase 3

**Study Design:** The trial is structured as a Parallel Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) utilizing a 1:1:1 allocation ratio across three arms. Stratification by Center is incorporated into the design.

**Primary Endpoints:** The study employs dichotomous (proportion) endpoints assessed at Week 16. These endpoints measure the percentage response rates, defined as:
*   sPGA Clear/Almost Clear ($\ge$2-point decrease).
*   PASI-75 response.
The primary comparison is between TAK-279 and Placebo.

### 2. Statistical and Power Assessment

**Statistical Adequacy:** The protocol summary currently assesses the trial as Adequately Powered to detect the target effect size ($\ge$2-point improvement) with 80% power ($\alpha=0.05$).

**Power Calculation Constraint:** A precise sample size calculation could not be formally performed due to the absence of necessary variance and baseline response rate parameters within the protocol summary.

**Current Enrollment:** The trial is currently enrolling 693 patients, resulting in 231 patients per arm.

### 3. Safety and Mechanism Profile

**Safety Monitoring:** Safety data will be collected through investigator reporting, supplemented by sponsor oversight. No Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) is currently planned.

**Safety Concerns:** A known class effect associated with the investigational agent necessitates vigilance regarding potential elevated Aspartate Aminotransferase (AST) levels.

**Mechanism of Action:** The drug functions as a TYK2 inhibitor.

**Pharmacogenetics:** No specific genetic biomarker screening criteria have been identified. The screening burden is primarily clinical, focusing on disease state and duration.

### 4. Feasibility and Recruitment Analysis

**Estimated Screen-to-Enrollment Yield:** The estimated screen-to-enrollment yield is classified as Moderate, projected between 5% and 20%. This yield is influenced by the stringent clinical criteria required, specifically the need to identify patients with:
*   A diagnosis of moderate-to-severe Plaque psoriasis.
*   Sufficient disease duration ($\ge$6 months).
*   Exclusion criteria, including the absence of recent infection history and prior TAK-279 exposure.

**Impact of Criteria Relaxation:** Simulation results indicate that relaxing specific eligibility criteria can significantly improve recruitment feasibility:
*   **Relaxing Recent Infection History:** This adjustment increases the estimated yield to 1.15 (115%), shifting the assessment to Moderate-High.
*   **Relaxing Disease Severity:** Allowing for mild-to-moderate disease increases the estimated yield to 1.30 (130%), classifying the assessment as High.

### 5. Conclusion and Strategic Recommendations

The trial exhibits a feasible structure based on its current enrollment numbers and statistical assessment. However, recruitment remains the primary variable influencing feasibility.

**Recommendations:**

1.  **Data Validation:** The highest priority is the immediate collection of variance and baseline response rate data to formally validate the biostatistician's assessment of the trial's power.
2.  **Recruitment Strategy Refinement:** Efforts should be focused on expanding the eligible patient pool by targeting individuals who meet core disease criteria but who may be borderline on exclusionary criteria (e.g., those with minimal recent infection history or mild-to-moderate disease).
3.  **Safety Monitoring:** Maintain stringent monitoring of safety endpoints, particularly related to AST elevation, given its established classification as a class effect.
