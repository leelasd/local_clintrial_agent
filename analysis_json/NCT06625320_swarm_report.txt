**Trial Title:** Phase 3 Study of Daraxonrasib (RMC-6236) in Patients With Previously Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma (PDAC)
**Trial Phase:** PHASE3

---

#### 1. Executive Summary

The feasibility analysis for NCT06625320 indicates a **Moderate recruitment yield**, estimated between **5% and 20% screen-to-enroll**. The eligibility criteria are generally comprehensive, focusing on a well-defined, homogeneous population suitable for a Phase 3 trial. The primary feasibility challenge lies in the strict requirement for **RAS mutation status**, which has a high potential for screen failure. Relaxing the criteria related to RAS testing and potentially certain prior treatment history could significantly improve enrollment rates.

#### 3. Eligibility Criteria Feasibility Analysis

The eligibility criteria are categorized below, highlighting feasibility, safety, and statistical power considerations.

**A. High-Impact/Restrictive Criteria (Primary Bottlenecks):**

1.  **RAS Mutation Status:**
    *   **Criterion:** Documented RAS mutation status (KRAS, NRAS, or HRAS at codons 12, 13, or 61).
    *   **Feasibility Impact:** **High Risk.** The protocol notes a population prevalence of $\sim30\%$ for the relevant mutation, translating to an **estimated screen failure rate of $\sim70\%$** based on the specific genetic check. This is the single largest potential barrier to enrollment.

2.  **Prior Therapy:**
    *   **Criterion:** Prior therapy with direct RAS-targeted therapy (e.g., degraders and/or inhibitors).
    *   **Feasibility Impact:** **Moderate Risk.** This criterion will exclude patients who have received prior targeted therapy, potentially limiting the eligible patient pool, especially in a Phase 3 setting where treatment history is common.

**B. Standard/Moderate Impact Criteria:**

*   **Disease Status:** Histologically/cytologically confirmed PDAC with metastatic disease; Measurable disease per RECIST 1.1. (Standard for oncology trials).
*   **Performance Status:** ECOG 0 or 1. (Standard for patient safety/tolerance).
*   **Organ Function:** Adequate organ function (bone marrow, liver, kidney, coagulation). (Standard safety check).
*   **Compliance/Logistics:** Able to take oral medications; Any conditions affecting study treatment absorption; Major surgery within 4 weeks prior to randomization; Patient is able to comply with protocol-required study visits. (These are common logistical filters).

#### 4. Relaxation Scenarios & Recommendations

To mitigate the recruitment yield risk, the following relaxation scenarios are recommended for consideration by the protocol team:

| Scenario | Criterion Relaxed | Proposed Change | Expected Impact on Yield | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Scenario 1 (High Impact)** | RAS Mutation Status | Allow enrollment of patients with **wild-type** RAS status, or consider a sub-study for non-mutant patients. | **High Increase** (Potentially 20-30% lift). | Medium |
| **Scenario 2 (Medium Impact)** | Prior RAS-Targeted Therapy | Allow patients with prior therapy, but require documentation of a specific washout period or specific prior agent. | **Moderate Increase** (Potential 5-10% lift). | Low |
| **Scenario 3 (Low Impact)** | Age/ECOG | Slightly widen the acceptable ECOG score (e.g., to 2) or include a broader age bracket if clinically justifiable. | **Low Increase** (Minimal). | Low |

---

#### 5. Conclusion and Next Steps

The trial design is statistically robust (as indicated by the power analysis provided in the protocol details). The primary feasibility concern is the **RAS mutation screening bottleneck**.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize investigating **Scenario 1 (RAS Relaxation)**. A targeted effort to enroll patients who are RAS wild-type (if clinically appropriate for the hypothesis) could significantly increase the achievable enrollment count from the target of 500 patients.

**Handoff:** This report is synthesized. The next step should be a focused discussion with the **swarm_coordinator** and **protocol_extractor** to implement the recommended relaxations and re-run the yield simulation to quantify the potential enrollment increase.
