**Clinical Trial Design Assessment Report: NCT06088043**

This report synthesizes the findings from the protocol extraction, statistical power calculation, and feasibility simulation phases for trial NCT06088043.

### 1. Protocol and Drug Structure Summary (Protocol Extractor Findings)

**Trial Identification:** NCT06625320 (Phase 3 Study of Daraxonrasib (RMC-6236) in Patients With Previously Treated Metastatic Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma (PDAC))
**Trial Design:** Parallel Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT) comparing the study drug against Standard of Care.
**Indication:** Metastatic Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma (PDAC).
**Primary Endpoint:** Progression-Free Survival (PFS) in the RAS G12-mutant population.
**Key Eligibility Criteria Summary:**
*   **Safety:** ECOG $\le 1$, adequate organ function (bone marrow, liver, kidney, coagulation), and history of prior RAS-targeted therapy are required.
*   **Statistical Power:** Patients must have histologically or cytologically confirmed metastatic PDAC and measurable disease per RECIST 1.1. A specific RAS mutation status (KRAS, NRAS, or HRAS at codons 12, 13, or 61) is required for the primary endpoint analysis.
*   **Feasibility:** Requirements include age $\ge 18$, ability to take oral medications, and exclusion of major surgery within 4 weeks prior to randomization.
*   **Pharmacogenetics Note:** The study targets RAS multi-inhibitors, focusing on KRAS, NRAS, and HRAS mutations. A significant screening challenge is noted, with an estimated $\sim 70\%$ screen failure rate for the required RAS mutation biomarker.

### 2. Statistical Power Analysis (Biostatistician Findings)

The analysis was based on a two-sided log-rank test to assess the difference in Progression-Free Survival (PFS) between the treatment arm and the control arm.

**Power Metrics:**
*   **Statistical Goal:** To achieve $80\%$ statistical power ($\beta = 0.20$) with a significance level ($\alpha$) of $0.05$.
*   **Required Sample Size (R-exact):**
    *   **Total Required Sample Size:** 262.35 patients.
    *   **Required Events:** 245.59 events.
*   **Clinical Interpretation:** The trial's design appears **Adequately Powered (R-exact)** based on the required sample size calculation. The anticipated Hazard Ratio (HR) reduction is $24\%$, with an estimated control median PFS of 3.5 months and an implied treatment median PFS of 4.6 months, indicating a clinically meaningful improvement.

### 3. Feasibility and Recruitment Yield Simulation (Feasibility Specialist Findings)

The simulation was run to assess the expected patient enrollment yield based on the protocol's eligibility criteria.

**Recruitment Yield Simulation:**
*   **Baseline Yield:** $1.0$ (100% conversion from screened eligible patients).
*   **Relaxation Scenarios:** No relaxation scenarios were modeled, suggesting the current criteria are strictly defined.
*   **Estimated Recruitment Yield:** **Moderate (5-20% screen-to-enroll)**.

**Feasibility Score:** The protocol summary assigned a Feasibility score of 5/5, reflecting a high degree of rigor in defining inclusion/exclusion criteria, though the genetic requirement presents a high barrier to entry.

***

### Synthesis and Overall Recommendation

The trial NCT06088043 is a well-defined, Phase 3, parallel RCT designed to test the efficacy of a RAS multi-inhibitor in patients with metastatic PDAC.

**Strengths:**
1.  **Sound Statistical Design:** The trial is adequately powered to detect a clinically meaningful difference in PFS ($24\%$ HR reduction) under the specified conditions.
2.  **Clear Endpoint Focus:** The primary endpoint (PFS in the RAS G12-mutant population) is specific and clinically relevant.
3.  **Rigorous Criteria:** Inclusion/exclusion criteria are comprehensive, addressing safety (ECOG, organ function, prior therapy) and statistical homogeneity (RAS mutation status).

**Key Risks and Considerations:**
1.  **Genetic Barrier:** The reliance on a specific RAS mutation status poses the single largest recruitment risk, with an estimated screen failure rate of $70\%$ for this biomarker. This will significantly impact the feasibility of reaching the target sample size of 500 patients.
2.  **Recruitment Rate:** The expected screen-to-enroll yield of $5-20\%$ is low and must be factored into the timeline and budget planning.

**Conclusion:**
The trial is statistically sound and methodologically rigorous. Successful execution hinges critically on the ability of the site staff and screening processes to navigate the highly specific genetic biomarker requirement while maintaining a realistic patient flow rate.
